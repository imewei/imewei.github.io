Research Projects

1. Adaptive and Dynamic Nanocomposites

The lab develops materials combining polymers and nanofillers through reversible bonds that can change shape, stiffness, or conductivity in response to heat, stress, or electric fields. These self-healing, recyclable composites incorporate nanoparticles, nanoclays, and fibers for enhanced mechanical and thermal properties.

Specialized polymer matrices—including polyampholytes, zwitterionic polymers, and polyionic liquids—enable applications in biomedical devices, soft robotics, energy storage, and smart infrastructure.

2. Non-Equilibrium Physics

This research examines how microscopic structure influences material performance in adaptive networks. Scientists use real-time X-ray techniques like Rheo-SAXS-XPCS to track how nanostructures and particle dynamics evolve during deformation.

The work captures bond breaking/reformation and cooperative particle movement under stress, supporting predictive models for durable, responsive materials.

3. Sustainable Biocomposites

Research focuses on replacing traditional plastics with natural fibers and bio-based polymers. The team develops biodegradable packaging using biomass waste through a simple, solvent-free process, incorporating dynamic covalent networks to improve mechanical strength, thermal resistance, and barrier properties for extended food shelf life.

4. Hetero-Charged Polymers

These materials address nonspecific adsorption challenges in water treatment. Hetero-charged polymers with cationic and anionic side groups resist protein adsorption and biofilm formation at solid-liquid interfaces, studied using in situ shearing X-ray systems.

5. Redox Gating

This technique modulates charge carriers exceeding 1016 cm-2 for precise electronic control in semiconductors. It operates at sub-volt levels without structural damage, enabling reversible metal-insulator transitions in materials like WO3 and VO2 for energy-efficient electronics.
